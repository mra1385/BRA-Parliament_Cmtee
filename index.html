Part One: General Provisions

Article 1: Definitions

For the purpose of applying the provisions of this law, the following definitions and terms shall be adopted:

  • Banque du Liban: The Central Bank established by the law enacted by Decree No. 13513 dated 1/8/1963 (Code of Money and Credit and the establishment of the Central Bank).
  • Higher Banking Commission: Article 10 of Law No. 28 dated 9/5/1967 and its amendments (amending and completing the legislation related to banks and establishing a mixed institution for the guarantee of bank deposits) is amended to read as follows:
    The commission concerned with taking punitive decisions against violating financial institutions, restructuring and/or liquidation decisions, and supervising their implementation in accordance with the provisions of this law and the relevant applicable laws.
  • Banking Control Commission: The supervisory body established by Law No. 28/67 dated 9/5/1967 and its amendments.
  • Depositors: Natural or legal persons (i.e., individuals or companies) who enter into a bank deposit contract with a licensed bank, whereby they hand over a sum of money to the bank for the purpose of safekeeping or investment according to agreed-upon terms, with the bank’s obligation to return this amount upon demand or on a specific date, with or without interest.

  • Deposits:
    • a- Customer Deposits: (including certificates of deposit issued by the bank and owned by customers) belonging to natural and legal persons, other than financial sector institutions, and which are not included among the exempted liabilities according to the definitions contained in the applicable laws.
    • b- On-Balance Sheet Deposits: belonging to financial sector institutions and arising from credit agreements between said institutions, in their capacity as financial intermediaries, and their customers.
    • c- Cash Guarantees and Cash Margins: given as collateral for funded facilities (i.e., any on-balance sheet facilities) up to the limit of the amount utilized from the facilities.

Article 2: Preliminary Provisions

Articles 1 to 36 define the provisions upon which the bank resolution and liquidation process is based, and shall be considered effective upon the issuance of the Financial Regularity and Deposit Repayment Law.

Article 3: Objectives of this Law

In compliance with the Code of Money and Credit, this law aims to address cases of default, protect deposits during the liquidation and resolution process, and limit the use of public funds in the resolution process of any defaulting bank.

Article 4: Scope of Application of this Law

The provisions of this law shall apply to foreign banks or branches of foreign banks operating in Lebanon, as well as to Lebanese banks, including their branches abroad, unless the laws and regulations of the host countries provide otherwise.

Article 6: Obligations of the Members of the Higher Banking Commission to Avoid Conflicts of Interest

  • Each member of the Higher Banking Commission shall submit to the General Secretariat of this Commission a special declaration concerning their independence and the absence of a conflict of interest when performing their duties with respect to a bank, based on the criteria listed below. The deadline for submitting this declaration shall be one month from the date of publication of this law in the Official Gazette for current members, and one month from the date of appointment for new members. Each member must also, immediately, submit an updated declaration should any change occur in their relationship with any bank. The General Secretariat of the Commission is tasked with ensuring the proper implementation of this process.
  • Should any member of the Higher Banking Commission have any type of direct or indirect relationship that exposes them to a conflict of interest or affects their independence in any way when performing their duties with respect to a bank subject to this law, this member shall refrain from participating in any discussion or decision regarding the concerned bank and from exercising their voting rights in this regard. In this case, the Commission’s decisions shall be taken by a majority of the remaining members.
  • The following criteria are adopted in determining a member’s independence and the absence of a conflict of interest:
  • They shall not have been a shareholder in the bank or its related institutions in the two years preceding their appointment.
  • They shall not have held, in the two years preceding their appointment, the position of a member of the board of directors, senior management, or a consultant at the bank or its related institutions.
  • They shall not be a borrower from the bank or from its related institutions.

Article 7: Commencing the Process of Bank Resolution or Liquidation

  • The Second Chamber of the Higher Banking Commission is responsible for deciding to subject banks to one of the following two processes in accordance with the provisions of this law:

a- The Bank Resolution Process:
The Commission shall issue a resolution decision for the bank (a resolution decision) which includes, for each bank, the resolution tools to be applied, the measures that the bank must adhere to throughout the process of resolving its situation, and the time required to complete these measures.

b- The Liquidation Process:
The Commission shall issue a decision to strike the bank off Banque du Liban’s list of banks and a decision to liquidate the concerned bank.
The striking off of the bank shall be followed by a decision to appoint a liquidation committee.

  • The decision for resolution or the decision to strike off is made based on a final assessment report sent by the Banking Control Commission, which is based on an independent assessor’s report to the Higher Banking Commission regarding the necessity of liquidating the bank or rehabilitating it through resolution procedures, after justifying the reasons that led to its decision.
  • The process of bank resolution or liquidation is based on an evaluation aimed at determining the net value of the bank’s assets and the size of the losses. The resolution process is based on an evaluation prepared by the Banking Control Commission, which raises a recommendation to the Higher Banking Commission regarding the possibility of the bank’s failure and the necessity of taking remedial measures, provided that the evaluation is completed based on

Article 8: Notification and Publication of Decisions of the Higher Banking Commission

  • Regarding the bank resolution process:
    The Higher Banking Commission shall immediately notify the concerned bank of the resolution decision (including the resolution tools to be applied) and the decision shall be registered in the Commercial Register. The Higher Banking Commission shall publish a summary that does not include any details about the resolution tools for the concerned bank, along with a summary of the evaluation results, in the Official Gazette, in a widely circulated newspaper in Lebanon, and on the website of Banque du Liban.
  • Regarding the liquidation process:
    The Higher Banking Commission shall immediately notify the concerned bank of the decision to strike it off and the decision to appoint a liquidator or a liquidation committee, and the two decisions shall be registered in the Commercial Register. The two decisions, along with a summary of the evaluation results, shall be published in the Official Gazette, in a widely circulated newspaper in Lebanon, and on the website of Banque du Liban.
  • The Higher Banking Commission shall publish an annual report summarizing the progress made towards achieving its objectives within the scope of this law, and shall send a copy of this report to the Lebanese Parliament.

Article 9: Funding of the Higher Banking Commission

Banque du Liban shall bear the expenses of the Higher Banking Commission.

Chapter Three: The Independent Assessment Process

Article 10: Principles of Assessment and Appointment of Independent Assessors

  • The Banking Control Commission shall appoint independent assessors to conduct the assessment.
  • The assessment shall be based on international valuation standards and International Financial Reporting Standards, while taking into account the prudential requirements and directives issued by Banque du Liban.
  • The assessment shall be completed within the timeframe set by the Banking Control Commission.
  • The following eligibility criteria shall be considered when appointing independent assessors:
  • They must possess the required qualifications, specifically regarding the criteria of integrity, competence, and professional experience.
  • They must be independent and have no professional or personal relationship with the bank or its related institutions that could expose them to a conflict of interest.
  • Independent assessors, their partners in the partnership agreement, and any external parties they contract with shall, for the duration of their assignment and even after its completion, adhere to the regulations of banking and professional secrecy for the benefit of the concerned bank and its clients, with respect to the information they become privy to in the course of performing their duties.
  • Banque du Liban shall bear all costs of the appointment of the independent assessor(s).
  • The independent assessors shall submit their reports to the Banking Control Commission, with a copy to the bank, within the timeframe set by the Banking Control Commission.

  • Banque du Liban shall issue, based on a proposal from the Banking Control Commission, instructions and details regarding the features, selection, and qualification of the assessor(s), along with the means, tools, and details of the required assessment.

Article 11: Acknowledgment of Assessment Results

The bank’s failure to notify the Higher Banking Commission of any objection to the assessment results conducted by the independent assessors shall be considered an acknowledgment of the assessment results. In the event the bank objects to the assessment results for material reasons, it may notify the Higher Banking Commission in writing, with a copy to the Banking Control Commission, of the detailed reasons for its objection within a maximum period of 30 business days from the date of the assessment report’s issuance.

The Higher Banking Commission may, for legitimate reasons and after receiving a report from the Banking Control Commission, decide to conduct a new assessment, partially or fully, within a maximum period of one month. In this case, the Higher Banking Commission shall decide whether new independent assessors will be appointed, and the assessment results shall be final.

The Higher Banking Commission shall make its decision on the resolution of the bank based on the assessment report and any amendments it deems appropriate after reviewing the bank’s submission. In the case of liquidation, it shall make its decision in accordance with what is stated in Article 23.

Chapter Four: The Process of Rectifying the Bank’s Status

Article 12: Criteria for Failure or Potential Failure

  • The Banking Control Commission shall assess whether the bank has failed or is likely to fail, and whether any alternative measures, such as a recovery plan or any other regulatory intervention, could prevent the bank’s failure, based on one of the following criteria:
  • The bank’s failure or potential failure to comply with the minimum capital requirements in a timely manner.
  • The bank’s failure or potential failure to comply with the minimum liquidity requirements in a timely manner.

13

Page 14

  • The bank’s failure or potential failure to meet its obligations as they fall due.
  • The bank’s failure or potential failure to achieve and maintain profitability.
  • The bank’s failure or potential failure to comply with the conditions under which the license was granted, including a material breach of applicable laws or regulations.
  • In the event that the Banking Control Commission deems that the bank has failed or is likely to fail, it shall submit a recommendation to the Higher Banking Commission within a maximum period of one week from the date of its assessment.

Article 13: Bank Resolution Tools

  • The Higher Banking Commission may decide to apply any of the following bank resolution tools, either individually or in conjunction with other tools:
  • Bail-in.
  • Recapitalization of the bank by shareholders, the parent bank, or new investors.
  • Transfer of some or all of the bank’s assets, rights, and liabilities to another institution.
  • Merger with another bank.
  • The bank resolution tools shall be applied as specified in the resolution decision issued by the Higher Banking Commission for each bank, in accordance with the provisions of Article 7 of this law.

Article 14: General Principles Governing the Bank Resolution Process

(Suspension of this article due to its direct link to the Financial Regularity and Deposit Recovery bill)

The following principles shall apply in the implementation of the bank resolution process:

  • The hierarchy of own funds and creditors as specified in Annex No. 1 shall be observed.

14

Page 15

  • Own funds shall absorb all losses first, on a pro-rata basis within a single liability class, according to the hierarchy shown in Annex No. 1.
  • The bank’s creditors shall absorb losses on a pro-rata basis within a single liability class, according to the hierarchy shown in Annex No. 1.
  • Creditors within the same class shall be treated equally in accordance with Annex No. 1 (the principle of equal treatment).
  • Depositors shall be protected in accordance with what will be determined in the law on addressing the financial gap, which allows for restoring balance to the financial system in line with the objectives mentioned in Article 3 of this law. No creditor within a single class, as per Annex No. 1, shall, as a result of the application of bank resolution tools, be in a worse financial position than their assumed position in the event of the bank’s liquidation. In circumstances where it is established for a creditor and/or shareholder that their financial position upon the bank’s resolution is worse than their assumed financial position had a liquidation decision been made instead of a resolution decision in the context of this law, the creditors and/or shareholders may submit appeals to the special court mentioned in Article 29 of this law within a maximum period of three months from the date of publication of the resolution decision. In cases where the entitlement of the creditor and/or shareholder is proven, the special court shall determine the compensation amount and demand its payment from the bank.
  • Bail-in (reduction in value and/or conversion of liabilities to capital instruments): In the event bail-in is used as one of the bank resolution tools, this tool shall be applied to customer deposits based on the single depositor concept.
  • Bank resolution tools shall be used until the bank complies with the minimum requirements for the adequacy of own funds.
  • Certain liabilities are excluded from the mechanism of reduction or conversion to capital instruments, as indicated in Annex No. 1 (Excluded Liabilities).
  • The bank’s obligations related to contingent liabilities, including off-balance sheet guarantees and credits, shall be cancelled where possible without additional risks and costs, and the cash collateral provided for them shall be released.

15

Page 16

  • Derivatives contracts shall be terminated and the remaining related liabilities, if any, shall be considered as unsecured liabilities, if possible without additional risks and costs.
  • All temporary accounts shall be settled or cleared.
  • Netting shall be performed between funded facilities (i.e., on-balance sheet facilities) and the cash collateral and/or cash margins and/or the fair value of financial instruments (bonds, shares, etc., if any) provided as security for these facilities, up to the amount utilized from these facilities, provided there is a legally enforceable netting agreement and the netting criteria are met in accordance with International Accounting Standard 32. Amounts exceeding the utilized amount from the facilities shall be subject to the bank resolution process.

Article 15: Exiting the “Under Resolution” Status

  • The bank shall send a request to the Higher Banking Commission, with a copy to the Banking Control Commission, requesting to exit the “under resolution” status and justifying its eligibility to do so.
  • The Banking Control Commission shall assess the bank’s eligibility to exit the “under resolution” status, including the bank’s status, its ability to continue, and its compliance with the laws, regulations, and provisions mentioned in its resolution decision within the specified deadlines. The Banking Control Commission shall send its recommendation on this matter to the Higher Banking Commission within a reasonable timeframe, not to exceed one month from the date the request is complete.
  • The Higher Banking Commission shall decide, based on the recommendation of the Banking Control Commission, whether the concerned bank may exit the “under resolution” status and shall notify the bank of its exit. This decision shall be registered in the Commercial Register and published in the Official Gazette, in a widely circulated newspaper in Lebanon, and on the website of Banque du Liban.

Chapter Five: Powers of the Higher Banking Commission / Second Chamber

Article 16: Powers of the Higher Banking Commission

  • The Higher Banking Commission shall have authority over the bank and exercises its powers over both the bank individually and collectively, without the need for the approval of the bank’s shareholders, its creditors, its board of directors, its senior management, or any other party, and especially that the Commission’s decision is not contingent upon the approval of any other authority.
  • The powers of the Higher Banking Commission include the following:
  • Appointing a temporary manager, in cases where this is appropriate in accordance with the provisions of Chapter 8 of this law.
  • Dismissing or replacing members of the Board of Directors and/or senior management, or requesting the bank to do so.
  • Appointing one or more independent, non-executive board members, or requesting the bank to do so.
  • Objecting to the appointment of new members to the Board of Directors and/or senior management.
  • Approving the bank resolution plan prepared/updated periodically for each bank.
  • Imposing the necessary measures to remove obstacles to the feasibility of resolving the bank’s situation, including but not limited to:
    ^ Requesting the bank to sell assets, including but not limited to its participations abroad.
    ^ Requesting the bank to limit or suspend certain existing operations and business activities.
    ^ Requesting the bank to change its legal or operational structure, including but not limited to restructuring its ownership structure.
  • Initiating the bank resolution process and deciding on its duration as needed.
  • Imposing on the bank the application of resolution measures and tools.
  • Modifying the maturity of debt instruments and other bank liabilities (including deposits), or adjusting the interest rate, and/or the principal due on these and other instruments and liabilities, or the due date of the interest and/or principal as needed.

17

Page 18

  • Suspending the trading of listed capital instruments issued by the bank, where it applies, and/or postponing market disclosure requirements after coordination with the competent authority.
  • Imposing a temporary moratorium to suspend the effects of existing and future legal proceedings for a period of two months, extendable up to a maximum of 8 months when necessary.
  • Requiring third parties to continue providing essential services to the bank whose situation is being resolved, in accordance with the existing terms and conditions before the resolution of the bank’s situation.
  • Imposing a suspension of early termination rights by prohibiting the execution of contractual clauses related to finance lease, service, or license agreements, which allow the counterparty to the bank under resolution to perform a set-off, accelerate the payment of obligations, or terminate these contracts upon the commencement of the bank resolution process.

The suspension of early termination rights related to financial contracts must not exceed a period of 3 days.
The early termination rights enjoyed by the counterparty against the bank under resolution shall be preserved in the event of any default that is not related to the commencement of the bank’s resolution process, before or after the suspension period.

  • Prohibiting the payment of any dividends to holders of capital instruments or the distribution of other capital gains to them, and any type of payment to members of the board of directors and senior managers, other than the basic allocations paid to senior management.
  • Imposing the recovery of funds paid to members of the board of directors or senior management in a manner that violates the law or the circulars of Banque du Liban or good governance rules, for a period of 10 previous years.
  • Striking off the concerned bank from the list of banks of Banque du Liban for the cases specified in this law, and then appointing a liquidator or a liquidation committee in accordance with Article 24 of this law.
  • Filing a lawsuit in the name and for the account of the bank, or at the request of the bank or the temporary manager, before the competent Lebanese courts and/or any competent court in other countries, against major shareholders, members of the board of directors and senior management, and auditors, who held positions in the ten years preceding the date of the resolution decision, and this, if there are logical reasons to suspect their involvement in any civil or criminal offense.
  • Giving instructions to the liquidator/liquidation committee in case of liquidation (as determined by Article 25 of this law).

  • The Higher Banking Commission may decide, after obtaining a report from the Banking Control Commission, for legitimate reasons related to the discovery of factual errors in the assessment conducted by the independent assessor, to partially or fully re-evaluate the bank, through the same independent assessor or another independent assessor, within an expedited and reasonable timeframe. The results of the second assessment shall be final and binding.
  • The Higher Banking Commission may decide not to adopt the general principle of treating creditors within the same class equally, specifying the cases where this principle was not applied and providing a transparent explanation of the reasons, but only if it deems it necessary to contain the potential impact of a bank’s failure on the banking sector as a whole or to secure the maximum benefit for all creditors.
  • The Higher Banking Commission may, if necessary, carry out a reverse operation, partially or fully, for any reduction in value and/or for other resolution tools. In this case, the Higher Banking Commission may decide to increase the value of the liabilities of creditors and/or shareholders that should not be subject to reduction and/or other resolution tools for the bank. The reverse operation shall be carried out in accordance with Annex No. 1 (Hierarchy of Own Funds and Creditors).

Chapter Six: Powers of the Banking Control Commission

Article 17: Powers of the Banking Control Commission in the Context of the Bank Resolution Process

  • The bank whose situation is being resolved shall remain under the supervision of the Banking Control Commission.
    -In addition to the powers and responsibilities granted to the Banking Control Commission under other provisions of this law and under other laws, the Banking Control Commission shall do the following:
  • Prepare a provisional assessment of the bank and/or appoint an independent assessor to assess the bank.
  • Send a report to the Higher Banking Commission on the assessment results.

19

Page 20

  • Sending a recommendation to the Higher Banking Commission regarding the necessary measures, including the bank resolution tools that must be adopted, or the liquidation or resolution of the bank’s situation and proposing the necessary measures.
  • Following up on the implementation of the bank’s resolution process and sending periodic reports to the Higher Banking Commission regarding compliance or non-compliance with the resolution decision.
  • Conducting an assessment of the eligibility and suitability of new shareholders and the members appointed to the board of directors and/or senior management of the bank whose situation is being resolved.
  • Reviewing the reports of the temporary manager and sending any observations to the Higher Banking Commission when needed.
  • Preparing a resolution plan for each bank and sending it to the Higher Banking Commission.
  • The Banking Control Commission shall issue, as needed, technical instructions and requirements to ensure the proper application of its powers and responsibilities in accordance with this law.

Chapter Eight: The Temporary Manager

Article 19: Appointment of the Temporary Manager

  • The Higher Banking Commission shall, if it deems it appropriate, appoint a temporary manager for a pre-determined period that is renewable by this Commission. It may delegate to him specific or broad powers, including the powers to manage the affairs of the bank undergoing resolution and the powers of the board of directors. The temporary manager shall exercise any powers related to the resolution of the bank’s situation based on a written decision from the Higher Banking Commission, which defines, for each bank, the temporary manager’s powers, scope of work, duties, term of appointment, and obligations towards the Higher Banking Commission.
  • The decision to appoint the temporary manager, issued by the Higher Banking Commission, shall be registered in the Commercial Register, and published in the Official Gazette, in a widely circulated newspaper in Lebanon, and on the website of Banque du Liban.
  • The Higher Banking Commission shall determine the remuneration of the temporary manager, and the concerned bank shall bear the full expenses.
  • The Higher Banking Commission may, if it deems it necessary, dismiss the temporary manager at any time and may appoint a replacement for the remainder of his term.

Article 20: Qualifications of the Temporary Manager

The temporary manager must:

  • Possess extensive professional experience of not less than twenty years in the banking and financial fields, with administrative experience and in-depth knowledge of the applicable financial and banking laws and regulations.

  • Be independent of the bank whose situation is being rectified.

The independence criteria include the following:
* Not be, directly or indirectly, a shareholder in the bank or its related institutions in the five years preceding his appointment.
* Not have, directly or indirectly, held a position as a board member, senior manager, or advisor at the bank or its related institutions in the two years preceding his appointment.
* Not be a borrower from the bank or its related institutions for more than 100 thousand US dollars.
* Not be a depositor (more than 100 thousand US dollars) at the bank or its related institutions.
* Not have a kinship relationship up to the fourth degree with a shareholder or a member of the board of directors or senior management at the bank or its related institutions.
* Any other criteria that may negatively affect his independence, directly or indirectly.

  • In the event the bank participates in an operation, and it is found that the temporary manager has a fundamental interest or relationship with it (directly or indirectly), this operation may be exceptionally carried out only after prior written approval from the Higher Banking Commission.
  • Disclose to the Higher Banking Commission any interest or relationship that conflicts with the principle of independence and absence of conflict of interest, and in the event he refrains from rectifying the situation as required, the Higher Banking Commission shall have the authority to dismiss him.

  • The temporary manager shall have unrestricted management over the assets, offices, property, accounting books, and other records of the concerned bank, and he shall also have the right of access to all information necessary to perform his duties.

Chapter Nine: The Liquidation Process

Article 22: Objectives of Liquidation

The liquidation process is carried out with the aim of protecting financial stability and maximizing the value of the benefit for all creditors.

The provisions of Article 17 of Law No. 110 dated 7/11/1991 and its amendments (Financial Situation Rectification) related to the self-liquidation report of any bank shall apply. In the event a bank decides to conduct a self-liquidation, it must submit a request to the Higher Banking Commission showing that its assets cover all liabilities.

Chapter Ten: Miscellaneous Provisions

Article 31: Appeal of Decisions of the Higher Banking Commission

All decisions issued by the Higher Banking Commission are subject to appeal before the special court established by Law No. 110 dated 7/11/1991, and the principles of civil procedure related to the court of first instance shall be followed. The appeal before the competent court does not suspend the execution of the appealed decision.

Article 32: Non-compliance with the Provisions of this Law or Obstruction of Work

Any party that does not immediately comply with the provisions of this law or otherwise obstructs the work of the Higher Banking Commission or the temporary manager in the course of performing their duties shall be subject to a fine, the value of which is determined by the Higher Banking Commission, provided it is not less than 300 times the minimum wage in Lebanon. The Higher Banking Commission shall be the competent authority to determine whether there is a violation of its decisions or non-compliance with the provisions of this law. The proceeds of this fine shall be allocated to cover the expenses of the Commission related to the application of this law.

Article 33: Cross-Border Cooperation

The Higher Banking Commission shall communicate and cooperate, in accordance with legal frameworks, with the competent authority in the home country or in the host country and with the supervisory authority in the home country or in the host country through the exchange of non-publicly available information regarding the application of this law.

Article 34: Legal Immunity

In the context of performing their duties or carrying out any action or omission that occurred during the exercise of their powers and responsibilities noted in this law, no liability shall be incurred by the Higher Banking Commission, the Banking Control Commission, the temporary manager, and the liquidator/liquidation committee, unless a final judgment is issued proving that the action or omission resulted from bad faith or was the result of fraud, negligence, or gross error on their part. The Higher Banking Commission shall bear the compensation for the cost of legal claims filed against it or against any of its members, employees, or representatives, and the committee shall bear

All banks must have fulfilled the following:

  • The minimum liquidity requirements, on the basis that the bank’s assets at Banque du Liban are considered within book maturities with a rescheduling of deposit maturities by the bank.

Justifying Reasons

For six years, Lebanon has been facing a severe financial crisis that has deprived depositors of access to their deposits—the fruit of their life’s work in most cases—and has severely impacted the banking sector. The State’s responsibility to find appropriate solutions stems from two inseparable principles: concern for the rights of depositors and working to ensure the recovery of the banking sector so it can fully perform its credit role, finance the economy, and stimulate its growth.

Page 35

Justifying Reasons

Since addressing the financial, economic, and banking crises from which Lebanon is suffering, in addition to preserving the rights of depositors, requires legislative steps linked to three main reforms concerning banking secrecy, bank restructuring, and addressing the financial gap in a way that allows for the restoration of balance to the financial system,

Accordingly, the government has approved, as a first step, a draft law aiming to amend the banking secrecy law, as a necessary condition for accountability and for a precise understanding of the financial gap, with a retroactive effect for ten years from the date of submission of each request.

In preparation for establishing a law to restore financial balance and recover deposits, which would allow for restoring balance to the financial system, this draft law concerning the resolution of the situation of banks in Lebanon comes as a second step. It aims to establish a modern legal framework in accordance with the best international standards, a framework which is lacking in Lebanese banking legislation and is needed by the government, as well as by Banque du Liban, to deal with all financial crises, foremost among them the current, extremely damaging crisis for Lebanese citizens and Lebanon’s economy.

In addition to responding to this urgent need, the proposed draft law has been, and continues to be, called for by Lebanese legal professionals and by international institutions interested in assisting Lebanon.

These two steps will be followed, in the near future, by a third step, which consists of establishing a draft law on addressing the financial gap in a way that allows for restoring balance to the financial system and contributes to extricating Lebanon from the depths of the crisis it has experienced since 2019.

It should be noted that the implementation of this draft law, in the context of executing the three related steps mentioned above, is suspended until the Financial Regulation and Deposit Recovery Law is passed, as the latter is considered a necessary condition for restoring balance to the financial system.

Therefore, the government has prepared the attached draft law and, in submitting it to the esteemed Parliament, hopes for its approval.
